PROJECT IDEA

NEXT WEATHER - THE CLIMATE RADAR (Application)

MOTIVATION

Our world is looking different every decade, year, day and month now. The effects of climate change have seen homes swept away by floods, fires decimating crops, and nuclear fallout that destroys entire populations. Our access to information and help is needed more than ever. Clear and concise accessibility on survival is imperative soon. Like how to access those evacuation centres in a flood, where the fires are in our local areas and how to ensure we can protect ourselves from exposure. But why is our information, our forecasts, news, indexes and updates on these events, all in different places? These precious moments we may have to survive, mean we need one access point for information. Thus, the need for “Next Weather – The Climate Radar”. The monthly in-depth briefing on your local climate data is collated by local authorities, the community and government bodies. These will include the air index, emergency weather alerts including natural disasters, evacuation centres, and short guides on surviving natural and man-made disasters.

DESCRIPTION

With information scattered all over the Internet, we can struggle to find the correct website for the correct information promptly, with society shifting towards ease of access, this application aims to assist in this area. Let's look at one of the main ideas for this application, we're aiming to have industries that rely heavily on the weather, like farmers, use the information that we can easily provide them (Hughes & Gooday, 2022). As the 2020 State of The Climate report reiterates: “Australian rainfall is highly variable and is strongly influenced by drivers such as El Niño, La Niña, the Indian Ocean Dipole and the Southern Annular Mode. Despite this natural variability, long term trends are evident in Australia's rainfall record” (Climate change in Australia 2020). Therefore, providing accessible information on these variable weather events is imperative. These kinds of reports are vital for the Farming Industry, and we aim to bring them to the consumer with the most precise and clear information possible. Therefore, this application is greatly important for the well-being and security of our communities. So, what makes the application so important for the consumer? Let's look at what features we plan on bringing in. Firstly, flooding is a natural disaster but can also be classified as a man-made disaster because of Climate Change. Flooding can occur by weather events, such as heavy rainfall or storms. It can also happen by what is known as a “King Tide” (BOM), which is where the Moon's gravitational pull affects the oceans on the globe. Bush fires are another example, and they can be caused by natural ignition. For example, lightning strikes, or human error (accidental or on purpose). They can be aggravated by other weather events such as wind gusts, droughts, high temperatures and low humidity. A guide on these weather events enables the consumer to understand how much of a risk there may or may not be daily and contain information about what they can do if this occurs. Another example is air quality which is essential for the world to live and thrive. The air index allows for the population to determine if the quality of air that is present at their location is ideal to breathe. It will also track major airborne pollutants such as Ground-level ozone, carbon monoxide, sulphur dioxide and nitrogen dioxide (NSW Government, Air Quality Basics). Another aspect of the application is thunderstorms. These are natural and common events present in the world today, of which, early information can become available due to the data that can be gathered days in advance of the weather event with today's technology. Ease of access to this information allows the consumer to predict and plan based on the weather advice. Thunderstorms can be deadly for humans, particularly with Asthmatic conditions. Melbourne experienced what was known as an “Epidemic Thunderstorm Asthma” on November the 21st 2016, which resulted in the deaths of 10 people and thousands struggling to breathe (Wood, 2017). Early detection and awareness are vital for those suffering from asthma to avoid doing activities that could potentially put them in an area where 'grass pollen' may be high (Wood, 2017). With major weather events like flooding and bushfires occurring in Australia on a regular occurrence every year, most victims that are in the affected areas will be required to evacuate and seek refuge at an evacuation centre. Locating your local evacuation centre can be tricky, with relying on outsourcing the information via social media or government websites. The app plans to introduce this information reliably to the consumer, for a hassle-free experience. Further still, you may require tips and guides for survival in events such as Nuclear Explosions. A nuclear blast can cause a lot of damage to humans, for example, the bright flash can cause severe eyesight damage or loss, the shockwave can cause internal organ failure, and the heat from the explosion can burn your skin (CDC radiation emergencies 2018). Even still, nuclear fallout will give you severe (if not fatal) radiation poisoning (CDC radiation emergencies 2018). There are many tips and ways learnt over the years to give you the best chance at survival possible. Having locally saved government issues websites available for viewing on the app available, means the consumer has the best chance of preventing injury or death.

TOOLS AND TECHNOLOGY

Using the BOM website for weather information, RFS & SES for advice and the community for updated information for their local evacuation centre/s. Servers will be required to store and archive information for weather events and what may be common in a particular postcode. Due to the outsourcing of information, the application requires less equipment use such as weather stations. Artificial Intelligence can be used for automated information gathering.

SKILLS REQUIRED & OUTCOME

Due to the outsourcing of information for the weather application, the skills depend upon those we source from. For example, meteorologists will be required for advice and distribution of information for the application. The RFS, SES, local community and meteorology websites are outsourced skills that will be used within the application. However, there will need to be a team to run the application, deal with customer complaints and feedback, and perform software updates as required. Therefore, software application developers, customer service members and the intermediary will be required between stakeholders.
In conclusion, this application is designed to create information in the most accessible manner for the consumer. The issue we currently face is climate change and present the information we need to survive is too complex and difficult to decipher in an emergency. This application will help solve this by using clear, concise messages and push alerts for people who need this information quickly. This application will have future positive ramifications that can potentially save lives, and better the chance of people staying healthy throughout their lifetime.
